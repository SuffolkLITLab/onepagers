The Document Assembly Line

Overview

Suffolk LIT Lab's Document Assembly Line is a system for building expert systems—online guided interviews for self-represented litigants to complete and file court forms and other documents.

Free Benefits

The Document Assembly Line is free and open source thanks to an active community of courts, legal aid organizations, and volunteers. It includes the following.

CourtFormsOnline

  • A user-friendly, mobile-first, customizable online interview portal for Docassemble interview servers.
  • Spot, the LIT Lab's AI issue spotter helps people find the form they need.
  • Built for courts and legal aid organizations.

E-Filing

  • An open-source e-filing service provider (EFSP).
  • Links Docassemble guided interviews with court e-filing management (EFM) systems, including Tyler's eFile & Serve.

Community Support

  • Learn to build guided interviews with Docassemble.
  • Weekly meetings, monthly workshops, and an online forum for solving problems and sharing knowledge.
  • Training, documentation, templates, and other resources.

Funded Partnership Benefits

Funded partnerships support the Document Assembly Line and enable us to provide additional services for our partners.

The Document Assembly Line is a toolbox for building online guided interviews for court forms and other legal documents for use by self-represented litigants. It is supported by the Suffolk LIT Lab and an active community of courts, orgs, and volunteers.

Quick Start

The Weaver tool generates draft interviews from Word and PDF documents. Also, take advantage of our library of pre-built questions, vetted by experts and translated into at least 5 languages. Building guided interviews takes time, but the Document Assembly Line includes some big shortcuts.

Community

Courts and orgs are already building with the Document Assembly Line and helping us maintain it. Suffolk Law students often work on court forms as part of their coursework, too.

Join our weekly community meetings to share knowledge among builders.

Open Source

The Document Assembly Line is open-source and community-built. No vendor lock-in, and no worries if a fire destroys the LIT Lab. Open-source software is digital infrastructure.

E-Filing Capable

The Document Assembly line integrates with Tyler’s e-filing management system, and custom integrations are possible.

FYI

Customizable

Use your own logo and color palette on your interviews.

Translatable

Your interviews can be translated into multiple languages.

Powered by Docassemble

The Document Assembly Line uses the free, open-source Docassemble software as its “engine.” Build with confidence knowing it will be free to use, forever.

Some Assembly Required

Interviews are based on readable, maintainable code. We can train anyone to build interviews as long as they are interested in learning.
