The Document Assembly Line is a toolbox for building online guided interviews for court forms and other legal documents for use by self-represented litigants. It is supported by the Suffolk LIT Lab and an active community of courts, orgs, and volunteers.

Quick Start

The Weaver tool generates draft interviews from Word and PDF documents. Also, take advantage of our library of pre-built questions, vetted by experts and translated into at least 5 languages.

Community

Courts and orgs are already building with the Document Assembly Line and helping us maintain it. Suffolk Law students often work on court forms as part of their coursework, too.

Join our weekly community meetings to share knowledge among builders.

Open Source

The Document Assembly Line is open-source and community-built. No vendor lock-in, and no worries if a fire destroys the LIT Lab. Open-source software is digital infrastructure.

E-Filing Capable

The Document Assembly line integrates with Tyler’s e-filing management system, and custom integrations are possible.

FYI

Customizable

Use your own logo and color palette on your interviews.

Translatable

Your interviews can be translated into multiple languages.

Powered by Docassemble

The Document Assembly Line uses the free, open-source Docassemble software as its “engine.” You can build with confidence knowing it will be free to use, forever.

Some Assembly Required

Interviews are based on simple, readable, maintainable code. We can train anyone to build interviews as long as they are willing to.
